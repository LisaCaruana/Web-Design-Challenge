British Invasion: Colonial History

The Candacraig Hotel in Pyin Oo Lwin, mountain retreat for British officers.

(Credit: Author)

One of the most resource-rich countries in the world, the lands and rivers that comprise modern-day Myanmar were highly coveted by the British Crown. A series of three Anglo-Burmese wars finally resulted in the British Navy overthrowing King Thibaw and the absorption of the kingdom into the British Empire, where it would remain until after World War II.
